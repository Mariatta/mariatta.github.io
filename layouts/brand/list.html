Logo redesign — handoff spec

A new monogram needs two SVG variants and a favicon. The colors and slot dimensions below are locked so the rest of the site doesn't have to change when the mark does.

Required deliverables

  • assets/images/site/brand-mark-light.svg — mark for light backgrounds
  • assets/images/site/brand-mark-dark.svg — mark for dark backgrounds
  • assets/images/site/favicon.svg — favicon, ideally theme-aware via prefers-color-scheme
  • assets/images/site/og-default.svg — 1200×630 social card; export to PNG with rsvg-convert for production

Format and dimensions

  • SVG, square viewBox 0 0 64 64 (favicon and brand mark)
  • Render area should occupy roughly 75–85% of the viewBox; leave 8–10 units of clearspace inside the canvas
  • Max two colors total: a primary (text or rose) and an accent (rose dot or detail)
  • No background fill on the SVG — mark must sit on whatever surface it's placed on
  • The navbar renders the SVG at ~42px height; the favicon renders at 16–32px. Verify legibility at both

Color usage

  • Light variant — primary stroke/fill: #1A1A1A (Text). Accent: #A8324A (Rose)
  • Dark variant — primary stroke/fill: #FAF7F2 (Background). Accent: #D97A90 (Rose lifted for dark contrast)
  • Do not introduce new colors. Stick to the tokens above

Voice cues for the mark

  • Editorial-with-warmth: serious enough for a Python core developer, soft enough for a personal blog
  • The rose accent should feel like a detail, not the headline — it's a spot of color, not the whole logo
  • Avoid: gradients, shadows, drop-shadows, photographic textures, gimmicks

Drop-in instructions

  1. Replace the four SVG files at the paths above
  2. Run hugo to verify the build is clean
  3. Open /, /about/, /projects/, and any post page to confirm the mark renders correctly in both light and dark modes
  4. Re-export og-default.png from the new SVG with rsvg-convert -w 1200 -h 630 -f png
